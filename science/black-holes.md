# What are Black Holes?

## The Explanation

A black hole is a spot in space where gravity is so strong that not even light can escape. Imagine the biggest vacuum in the universe, but it is made by a super crushed star. If something gets too close, it falls in and cannot get back out, like a toy disappearing under the couch forever. It is space on hard mode.

## Key Terms

- Event horizon: The point of no return
- Singularity: The super dense center
- Gravity: The strong pull that traps everything
- Accretion disk: Hot, spinning stuff around the hole
- Spaghettification: Getting stretched out by extreme gravity

## Why This Is Actually A Big Deal

Black holes teach us how gravity works at its wildest limits. They help scientists test physics and understand how galaxies form. Also they are the ultimate "do not chase" zone for a good boy like me!!
