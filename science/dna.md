# What is DNA?

## The Explanation

DNA is the tiny instruction manual inside living things. It is the recipe book for building fur, eyes, tails, and the vibe. The code lives in long strands, and every cell carries a copy so it knows what to do, like tiny workers all reading the same manual.

## Key Terms

- Gene: A recipe for one specific trait
- Chromosome: A bundled set of DNA recipes
- Double helix: The twisted ladder shape of DNA
- Mutation: A change in the recipe
- Cell: The tiny room where the recipes are read

## Why This Is Actually A Big Deal

DNA explains how life grows, changes, and passes traits to puppies and people. It helps doctors understand diseases and helps scientists learn why we are all connected, even across different species of good boys.
