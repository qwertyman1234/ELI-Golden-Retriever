# What is Blockchain?

## The Explanation

Blockchain is the group chat receipt chain for transactions. Every time someone says, "I traded a treat," a new line goes into a page. When the page fills, it gets sealed with a magic sniff stamp (a hash) and stapled to the next page, making a chain you cannot quietly edit. If a sneaky cat tries to change an old line, the stamp no longer matches and the whole pack goes OFF: "HEY, THAT SMELLS WRONG!!" Lots of computers keep copies, so no single human can rewrite the story.

## Key Terms

- Block: One diary page!! Full of secrets and treats and trades!!
- Chain: All the pages stapled together forever and ever
- Hash: The magic sniff-stamp that proves nobody messed with the page
- Node: A good boy computer keeping their own copy of the diary
- Consensus: When the WHOLE PACK agrees yes this page is real good job everyone

## Why This Is Actually A Big Deal

Blockchains let people share records without trusting one referee. That is useful for digital money and any system where a permanent, tamper resistant log matters. It is like a thousand synchronized watchdogs all barking, "Yep, that page is legit!!"
