# What are Stocks?

## The Explanation

Stocks are tiny ownership pieces of a company. If a company is a big dog park, a stock is like owning one small patch of grass. When the company does well, your patch can become more valuable, and sometimes you get a treat called a dividend. When the company does not do well, your patch shrinks in value. It is basically park ownership with market drama and number go up or down energy.

## Key Terms

- Share: One tiny piece of ownership
- Dividend: A payout treat from profits
- Market: The big place where shares are traded
- Price: What a share costs right now
- Risk: The chance your patch of grass gets less valuable

## Why This Is Actually A Big Deal

Stocks let companies raise money to grow, and they let people invest in that growth. They can build wealth over time, but they also come with real risk, so you have to be a thoughtful, not reckless, good boy.
